Planet Pesto

UI & Level Designer | June 2025 – July 2025

Project Overview

Planet Pesto is a Unity-based platformer game developed for IndieCade's 2025 Climate Jam. I served as UI and Level Designer, creating visual assets and designing engaging game levels that communicate climate action themes through gameplay.

Achievement: 14th place out of 34 entries in IndieCade's 2025 Climate Jam competition

My Role

  • Created 4 UI assets in Adobe Illustrator with a cohesive steampunk-inspired aesthetic
  • Co-designed 4 platformer game levels through iterative design sprints
  • Led weekly team meetings to brainstorm, draft, and refine level designs
  • Incorporated peer and playtester feedback to improve user experience

Design Process

The UI design went through multiple iterations, exploring different menu layouts, pause screen designs, and in-game UI elements. The steampunk aesthetic was chosen to complement the game's environmental themes while maintaining clear usability.

Level design involved careful consideration of difficulty curves, player guidance, and environmental storytelling. Each level was playtested multiple times and refined based on feedback.

UI Design Explorations

Various menu and UI concepts were explored, from ornate steampunk frames to minimalist modern designs. The final designs balanced aesthetic appeal with functional clarity.

Key Learnings

This project taught me the importance of iterative design in game development, the value of playtesting early and often, and how to balance artistic vision with usability constraints in an interactive medium.

GreenWay

UX Researcher & Designer | February 2025 – March 2025

The Problem

Boston's MBTA Green Line commuters face numerous challenges including frequent delays, inconsistent train capacity, and difficulty navigating complex station layouts and surrounding areas. These pain points create stress and uncertainty for daily riders.

Research

I conducted user research to identify key commuter pain points:

  • Unpredictable delays without clear communication
  • Inconsistent train capacity leading to overcrowding
  • Navigation challenges within stations and surrounding areas
  • Lack of real-time service updates
  • Difficulty planning alternative routes during disruptions

The Solution

GreenWay is a mobile app designed to improve the Green Line commuter experience through real-time service updates, train capacity indicators, and enhanced wayfinding features including AR navigation within stations.

Key Features: Real-time delay notifications, train capacity visualization, station wayfinding, and route planning

Design Process

The design evolved through three major iteration cycles, each incorporating peer feedback and collaborative problem-solving. Early wireframes focused on information hierarchy, while later iterations refined the visual design and interaction patterns.

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of designing for real-world constraints and user stress. Features needed to be immediately accessible and understandable, especially during high-pressure commute situations.

Sleep Survey

UX Researcher & Designer | March 2025 – April 2025

Project Overview

Sleep Survey is a dream documentation and analysis app designed to help users improve their sleep quality and overall wellbeing through structured journaling and AI-powered dream analysis.

Research Insights

My research explored sleep-related stressors and their impact on wellbeing:

  • Psychological factors affecting sleep quality
  • Impact of major life changes on sleep patterns
  • Positive effects of sleep journaling on rest quality
  • User interest in understanding dream patterns and meanings
Key Finding: Regular sleep journaling showed significant positive impact on users' ability to identify stressors and improve sleep habits

Design Solution

The app features a calming purple color scheme designed to promote relaxation. Key features include a dream calendar for tracking patterns, journal entries with AI analysis, and personalized insights about sleep quality over time.

Iteration Process

The prototype went through three iteration cycles. Early feedback revealed users wanted more guidance on what to journal about, leading to the addition of prompts and structured entry options. Later iterations focused on making the AI analysis more accessible and actionable.

Reflections

Designing for wellness and mental health required careful consideration of tone, accessibility, and user emotional state. The project taught me the importance of creating safe, non-judgmental spaces in digital products.
